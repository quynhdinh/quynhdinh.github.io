Today marks one year since I moved to the U.S.
Here is the timeline of my life in the past year. I landed in the U.S on April 25, 2025. I started taking classes one after another until December. I was then allowed to look for jobs. I spent 2 months looking for jobs, at the time I also wanted to travel around. My friend told me he was moving to Dallas, Texas, and tell me if I want to go with him. I said yes and we moved to Dallas in February 2026. Things don't go well in Dallas, my mental health was not good, I reached out to my cousin in Tampa, Florida. He found me a roomate and I moved to Tampa in late Febrary. After a month in Tampa, I got myself a job. I started working in March 26th, 2026.

Here is a few things I learned about U.S. culture in the past year:

1. Individualism: People in the U.S. value individualism and independence. This one struck me the most. I have heard about this before I came to the U.S., but I didn't expect it to be this strong.

2. The culture of work: People in the U.S. are very hardworking and they take their work seriously. They also value efficiency and productivity.

3. Americans like to be friendly and like to joke.

Coming to 2026. There is kind of one goal I set for myself in 2026, which is to be more risky. I want to try new things and step out of my comfort zone. I want to be more spontaneous and not overthink things. I want to be more open-minded and not be afraid of failure. I want to be more adventurous and not be afraid of trying new things.
